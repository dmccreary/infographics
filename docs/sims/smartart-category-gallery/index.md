## About This MicroSim

Microsoft's SmartArt system organizes diagrams into eight fundamental categories that cover the most common ways information is visually structured. This gallery presents each category as a clickable thumbnail card with a miniature visual representation drawn in real time. Click any card to see a detailed description and a list of common subtypes.

Understanding these eight categories gives you a shared vocabulary for discussing diagram types and helps you quickly select the right visual structure for your content.

## Lesson Plan

### Learning Objective

Students will be able to identify the eight SmartArt categories by their visual thumbnail and name, building recognition of the standard diagram vocabulary.

### Activities

1. **Gallery exploration**: Click each of the eight cards and read the description. Which categories are most familiar to you?
2. **Category matching**: For each of these content types, identify the best SmartArt category: a company org chart, a product development workflow, a comparison of three competing frameworks, a food pyramid.
3. **Subtype discovery**: Explore the subtypes listed for each category. How do subtypes within the same category differ from each other?

### Assessment

- Name all eight SmartArt categories from memory.
- Given a content description, select the most appropriate category and justify your choice.
