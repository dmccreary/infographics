# Prokaryote vs. Eukaryote Cell Comparison — Image Prompt

## Purpose

Generate a single wide illustration showing a prokaryotic bacterial cell on the left and a eukaryotic animal cell on the right, side by side in a single frame. The image will be used as a background for an interactive MicroSim where students hover over numbered callout markers to learn about each structure. **No text, labels, numbers, arrows, callout lines, leader lines, or annotation marks anywhere in the image.**

## Global Specifications

- **Aspect ratio:** 4:3 landscape (1200 × 900 px)
- **Style:** Clean biological textbook illustration, precise molecular-level artwork with flat color fills, white background, AP Biology high school level
- **Layout:** A single continuous scene with two cells. The prokaryotic cell occupies the left ~40% of the image. The eukaryotic cell occupies the right ~55%. A narrow gap of white space (~5%) separates them.
- **Background:** Pure white
- **No text of any kind in the image. No labels, no cell type names, no chemical formulas, no numbers, no arrows, no annotation marks. Repeat: absolutely no text.**

## Prokaryotic Cell (left side)

Draw a **large oval bacterial cell** filling the left portion of the image, oriented slightly diagonally (tilted ~15 degrees from horizontal).

- **Cell wall:** Thick outer boundary (8-10 px) in tan/sandy brown (#D4A574). Clearly distinguishable from the plasma membrane inside it.
- **Plasma membrane:** Thinner inner boundary (4-5 px) in medium blue (#5DADE2), just inside the cell wall with a narrow gap of white between them.
- **Cytoplasm:** Light yellow-green (#F9F3D0) fill inside the plasma membrane.
- **Nucleoid region:** An irregular, cloud-like zone of teal blue (#1ABC9C) in the center of the cell, NOT enclosed by any membrane. Should look like tangled DNA strands/fibers spreading through the central area, occupying ~25% of the cell interior. The boundary should be diffuse/fuzzy, not sharp — emphasizing that there is NO membrane around it.
- **Circular DNA (chromosome):** Within the nucleoid, show one large tangled circular loop of teal DNA.
- **Plasmid:** One small separate teal ring (#16A085) near the edge of the nucleoid, clearly a separate small circle of DNA distinct from the main chromosome.
- **Ribosomes (70S):** Many tiny dark gray dots (#555555) scattered throughout the cytoplasm, ~40-50 dots visible. They should be SMALL (3-4 px each) to contrast with the larger eukaryotic ribosomes.
- **Flagellum:** A single wavy/sinusoidal dark green (#27AE60) line extending from the right end of the cell, about 40% of the cell's length. It should look whip-like and flexible.
- **Pili:** 6-8 short olive green (#7D8B3E) stubby projections distributed around the outer surface of the cell wall, much shorter than the flagellum (~10-15% of cell width).
- **No membrane-bound organelles.** The interior should be clearly simpler than the eukaryotic cell — just ribosomes, nucleoid DNA, and cytoplasm.

## Eukaryotic Animal Cell (right side)

Draw a **large irregular rounded cell** filling the right portion of the image. The shape should be slightly amoeboid — not a perfect circle, with some gentle bumps and indentations.

- **Plasma membrane:** Thin boundary (4-5 px) in medium blue (#5DADE2). No cell wall.
- **Cytoplasm:** Pale peach/cream (#FDEBD0) fill.
- **Nucleus:** A large oval structure (~20% of cell diameter) positioned slightly off-center (upper left quadrant of the cell). Dark blue (#2C3E80) fill with a clearly visible double-membrane nuclear envelope (#1A237E, two parallel lines with nuclear pores shown as small gaps).
- **Nucleolus:** A small dense deep purple (#6C3483) circle inside the nucleus, ~25% the diameter of the nucleus.
- **Rough ER:** A network of blue-purple (#7E57C2) folded membranes extending from the nuclear envelope toward the cell interior. Show 4-5 parallel wavy membrane sheets with dark gray dots (ribosomes) studding the outer surface. Should visually connect to the nuclear envelope.
- **Smooth ER:** A smaller network of light purple (#B39DDB) tubular membranes near the rough ER but WITHOUT ribosome dots. Should look smoother and more tubular.
- **Golgi apparatus:** 4-5 stacked gold/amber (#F5A623) curved cisternae (like a stack of pita bread) with small vesicles budding from the ends. Position in the lower-center area of the cell.
- **Mitochondria:** 2-3 red-orange (#E74C3C) oval organelles with visible internal cristae (folded inner membrane). Each ~8% of cell diameter. Distribute in different areas of the cytoplasm.
- **Ribosomes (80S):** Dark gray dots (#444444) scattered in the cytoplasm, slightly LARGER than the prokaryotic ribosomes (4-5 px each). Also visible on the rough ER surface.
- **Lysosomes:** 2-3 small brown-orange (#D35400) spheres scattered in the cytoplasm, each ~4% of cell diameter.

## Composition Notes

- Both cells should be drawn at approximately the same visual prominence — the prokaryote should not look tiny next to the eukaryote
- The eukaryotic cell should be ~30% larger than the prokaryotic cell to suggest the actual size difference, but both should fill their respective halves
- The complexity difference should be visually obvious: the prokaryote has a simple interior (DNA + ribosomes), while the eukaryote has many distinct compartmentalized organelles
- Each structure must occupy enough image area that a 24 px circular marker dot can be placed on it without touching neighboring structures
- Use flat color fills with very subtle shading — no heavy gradients, no photorealistic rendering
- The style should be consistent between both cells — same line weights, same artistic approach

**No text, labels, numbers, arrows, callout lines, leader lines, or annotation marks anywhere in the image. This is critical — the interactive overlay adds all labels programmatically.**
